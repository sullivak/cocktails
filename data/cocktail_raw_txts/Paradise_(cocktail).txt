The '''Paradise''' is an [[IBA official cocktail]], and is classified as a "pre-dinner" drink, an [[apéritif]].

The earliest known in-print recipe for the Paradise Cocktail was written by [[Harry Craddock]] in 1930. This cocktail is prepared using [[gin]], [[apricot brandy]] (apricot liqueur), and orange juice in a 2:1:1 ratio, with a splash of lemon juice.<ref>{{cite book
|author=Harry Craddock
|title=The Savoy Cocktail Book
|publisher=Constable & Company, London
|year=1930
|isbn=1-86205-296-4
}}</ref>

==In popular culture==
The Paradise cocktail plays a key part in the 1933 [[Warner Bros.]] romantic film ''[[One Way Passage]]'' as the drink of the two star-crossed lovers played by [[Kay Francis]] and [[William Powell]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.tcm.com/mediaroom/video/292668/One-Way-Passage-Movie-Clip-Paradise-Cocktail.html|title=One Way Passage -- (Movie Clip) Paradise Cocktail|website=Turner Classic Movies}}</ref>

On May 27, 2018, [[Snoop Dogg]] set the world record for the largest "Gin and Juice", a 500 litre paradise cocktail.<ref name="Snoop">{{cite web |title=New record: Largest Paradise cocktail |url=https://twitter.com/GWR/status/1001090490220122118?ref_src=twsrc%5Etfw |website=Twitter |publisher=Guinness Book of Records |accessdate=30 May 2018}}</ref>
